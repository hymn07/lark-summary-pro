## The Problem with Email

Every day, millions of business-critical decisions are trapped inside email threads. Approvals sit unread, project updates get buried, and important deadlines slip through the cracks.

Despite the rise of task management tools, CRMs, and collaboration platforms, **email remains the backbone of business communication**. The average professional spends 28% of their workday reading and responding to emails — that's over 2 hours every single day.

### Email Is a Communication Tool, Not an Execution Engine

The fundamental problem isn't email itself — it's that email was designed for *communication*, not *execution*. When your CEO sends an approval request, when a team member submits a weekly report, or when a client raises an issue, these emails represent **business actions** that need to be tracked, processed, and resolved.

But in a traditional inbox, they're just text. No structure. No status tracking. No automated follow-up.

## Our Vision: Email as a Business Execution Layer

Flowmail sits on top of your existing inbox (Gmail, Outlook) and transforms incoming emails into **structured, trackable, and executable business entities**.

Here's how it works:

1. **Connect** — Link your email account via OAuth. We never store your password.
2. **Understand** — AI classifies each email and extracts structured data: who's asking, what they need, when it's due.
3. **Execute** — Take action directly: approve requests, track report progress, update statuses — all with full audit trails.

### Human-in-the-Loop by Design

We believe AI should augment human judgment, not replace it. Every AI classification and extraction in Flowmail is presented for **user confirmation** before any action is taken. You stay in control while AI handles the heavy lifting.

## What's Next

We're starting with two core scenarios:

- **Approval Workflows** — Turn approval request emails into trackable items with one-click approve/reject
- **Report Tracking** — Automatically extract progress updates and flag risks from team reports

Our goal is simple: **stop reading emails, start executing.**
